CyberPatriots

Was established in 2009.

This club is sponsored by the Air Force Association, CyberPatriot is the National Youth Cyber Education Program. Competitions put teams of middle and high school students in the position of newly hired IT professionals. Competitors are given virtual images of an operating system and tasked with finding cybersecurity vulnerabilities within the images and hardening the system. Teams compete for top placement within their state and region for an opportunity to go to the National Competition.

Vex Robotics

Was established in 1989.

VEX Robotics allows students to build and design robots each year in order to accomplish a specific task. Students are encouraged to work as a team as they come up with creative solutions while problem solving those designed tasks. Robot builds MUST be designed and built by the students. Parents and teachers may assist, but they are not meant to take over the build. The robots must be made from VEX parts and are controlled with a remote. Students are encouraged to keep a journal during their build as this is part of the competition as well. A practice field is set up in the Engineering room (Woods) and may be used during FLEX Time. Competitions are held locally at Alliance Academy each month. Students will have the opportunity to qualify for State and National competitions by winning locally. For the 2020-2021 School Year, Mr. Woods and his Vex participants will be working towards becoming familiar with Vex as well as the process of designing and building a robot.

How Lakeside Middle School was Founded
We have been teaching kids since 2009.

Acoording to the lakeside website it says,"Lakeside Middle School opened in 2009. Approximately 730 students from Otwell, Riverwatch and South Forsyth middle schools joined to form this new school. Because of its close proximity to Lake Lanier, the school was named Lakeside. Lake Lanier is a reservoir that is named for poet Sidney Lanier. It was built by the U.S. Army Corps of Engineers in the 1950s and is used for hydroelectricity, flood control, drinking water and recreational purposes".
